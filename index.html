Timeline for Creation of 3D Printer

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    Our Humble Beginnings

    In the first engineering class at Brandeis University, Introduction to Design Methodology, this is our first project for the creation and assembly of 3D printers.

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    A 3D Printer is Born

    Shown in the "Portfolio" section above is the mini timeline of the creation of our 3D printer, which printed out a 3D boat in the end using a 3D benchy called Thingiverse. In the idealized 3D benchy, it has a single and multi-part STL for 3D printing the boat in one instance. The calibration and settings are as it comes when you open Prusa Slicer. Compared to the printed 3D benchy, it has very similar settings but the scale was lowered down to reduce the print time, which is a very good factor to do for future prints. To improve the calibration of the 3D printer settings, altering the perimeters and layer height to modify the item to print out well in the finish. Doing calibration to a 3D printer allows that specific item to come out the way you wanted it, every time.

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    Transition to Full Capacity

    In between the 3D printer setup, our 3D printer at first did not calibrate our filament correctly causing us to restart multiple times before it worked again. In addition, we used a different print bed, a stable surface for the object to be built on. Most of the other people used the current one on our 3D printer above, but the current one is more efficient in terms of what we needed to be done.

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    Final Level to Expansion

    Finally, after being calibrated multiple times and the extension of assembling the pieces together, the 3D printer was finally finished. It took around 2-3 hours to print our object. It came out very well and gave us the knowledge and ability to now know how to 3D print many more items whilst changing mini components of that specific object.

The Start of Something Cooler

This picture is an image of a freshly, 3D printed Arthiritis Typing Aid. An Arthiritis Typing Aid is a helpful tool in the shape of a sphere ball that you hold in the palm of your hand with a grip attached with an elongated piece on the end, which is the pointer to click on the keys of a keyboard of any device for people suffering from the disease of arthiritis. This is the first print from TinkerCad, then impleneted to PrusaSlicer with no modifications at all to test how well it does. I personally chose this item to be printed because I have one of my relatives who has arthiritis in their hands and it is very hard for them to type, or do anything, so this project is very personal to me.

Deepest

Soldering Video Time! ("Click the video play button in bottom left corner to see it play")

In this video, is a very well done demonstration from my Professor of how to solder. In one hand, he has a soldering iron and in the other hand is a roll of metal. He put the soldering iron in a bowl of tin to clean off the metal before applying more metal. Then, he got the metal and applied both together when merging it to the alarm clock, hence that the metal and soldering iron can come together and melt the piece connected to the alarm clock firmly. You do not want to put too much metal on the alarm clock because it could stick causing problems, so just apply a little bit for perfection on the clock. I soldered half of the clock, and my partner soldered the other half. I got the hang of it pretty well, I did it a different way before, but changed to the way my Professor did it to enhance cleanliness on the clock. This was my first time hearing or seeing anything like this before, so it was a very cool first experience.

INITIATION YES!!

TAKE OFF !

In this sketch, my partner and I were asked to pick a particular acitivity, it could be any like gardening or cycling for example, and we were asked to sketch a modified e-NABLE hand on paper with any changes that could allow functionality with the use of it. The sketch that I drew and my partner thought of with me is an illustration of the e-NABLE hand, and we chose the activity motor biking. We chose motor biking because it is a common but not so common activity these days, and we thought it would be a cool activity for this project. Right now, the fingers when you use the hand are straight upwards, but we wanted to modify that so when you use the hand as gloves during motor biking, they are clenched downwards so they fit perfectly when riding, and also, when you want them to be straight upwards you can choose to do it. The most important modification is about the threaded strings. Right now, the strings are put all the way through the back of the finger when threaded inside, but we wanted to change that so the fingers are more flexible to bend downwards. So, we would not put the string all the way through the back of the finger, just halfway through it so it is still able to function as needed.
