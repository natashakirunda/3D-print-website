About

Timeline for Creation of 3D Printer

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    Our Humble Beginnings

    In the first engineering class at Brandeis University, Introduction to Design Methodology, this is our first project for the creation and assembly of 3D printers.

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    A 3D Printer is Born

    Shown in the "Portfolio" section above is the mini timeline of the creation of our 3D printer, which printed out a 3D boat in the end using a 3D benchy called Thingiverse. In the idealized 3D benchy, it has a single and multi-part STL for 3D printing the boat in one instance. The calibration and settings are as it comes when you open Prusa Slicer. Compared to the printed 3D benchy, it has very similar settings but the scale was lowered down to reduce the print time, which is a very good factor to do for future prints. To improve the calibration of the 3D printer settings, altering the perimeters and layer height to modify the item to print out well in the finish. Doing calibration to a 3D printer allows that specific item to come out the way you wanted it, every time.

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    Transition to Full Capacity

    In between the 3D printer setup, our 3D printer at first did not calibrate our filament correctly causing us to restart multiple times before it worked again. In addition, we used a different print bed, a stable surface for the object to be built on. Most of the other people used the current one on our 3D printer above, but the current one is more efficient in terms of what we needed to be done.

  • Tues Sept 5th, 2023

    Final Level to Expansion

    Finally, after being calibrated multiple times and the extension of assembling the pieces together, the 3D printer was finally finished. It took around 2-3 hours to print our object. It came out very well and gave us the knowledge and ability to now know how to 3D print many more items whilst changing mini components of that specific object.

In-depth

Digging deeper into 3D printing!

The Liquid Crystal Display (LCD) grey ribbon and screen shown in orange are connected to the 3D printer. They are to allow calibration for whichever item of whomever's choice is printed on the 3D print bed. In this case, a boat is the item being printed from this calibration and print bed. It was hard at first to understand the connecting of the ribbon through the wires inside the printer, but eventually got it.

Transition

HUGE TRANSITION!

At this moment, the 3D printer is all assembled, connected and ready to be calibrated for usage. In this time, pink filament is being used. The red button in the far left corner is showing that the 3D printer is on and working. The machine is now getting ready to start working. On Prusa Slicer, seeing the digital copy of the boat, you then click slice now after fixing all the settings, later downloading and exporting the g-code to a flash drive plugged into your laptop. After it is exported, remove the flash drive out from the laptop and into the printer, which will then send the code all to the printer.
